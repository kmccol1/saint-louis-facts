Why Saint Louis?

Saint Louis, Missouri, is a city rich in history, culture, and unique attractions. From the iconic Gateway Arch to its vibrant food scene, there's always something interesting happening in this Midwestern gem. Explore these high-quality facts to learn more about this amazing city.

Fun Facts About Saint Louis

  • Gateway Arch: A National Landmark

    Saint Louis is home to the iconic Gateway Arch, the tallest man-made monument in the U.S. at 630 feet (192 meters). It's a symbol of the city's role in the westward expansion of the United States.

  • Historic Westward Expansion

    Saint Louis was the starting point for the Lewis and Clark Expedition in 1804. It played a crucial role in the westward expansion of the United States.

  • The Saint Louis Zoo

    The Saint Louis Zoo is one of the oldest and best zoos in the country, and it’s free to visit! It features over 12,000 animals and is located in Forest Park.

  • A Food Lover’s Paradise

    Saint Louis is famous for its distinctive food scene, including dishes like toasted ravioli, St. Louis-style pizza, and gooey butter cake.

  • Forest Park: A Natural Gem

    Forest Park is larger than New York's Central Park and is home to several cultural institutions, including the Saint Louis Art Museum, the Missouri History Museum, and the Saint Louis Science Center.

  • The First U.S. Bakery

    The first bakery in the United States was established in Saint Louis in 1794, and the city remains a center for bread-making traditions, especially in artisan and sourdough bread.

  • Home of Anheuser-Busch

    Saint Louis is the birthplace of Anheuser-Busch, the famous brewery responsible for beers like Budweiser. The iconic brewery tour is a must for beer lovers.

  • A City of Innovation

    Saint Louis has a history of innovation, from the first successful parachute jump in 1912 to the invention of the first robot-assisted surgery system in the 1990s.

  • Music and Culture Hub

    Saint Louis has long been a hub for music, from blues and jazz to rock and hip hop. The city hosts numerous music festivals, with the Saint Louis Symphony Orchestra being one of the oldest in the U.S.

  • World-Class Museums

    Saint Louis boasts a variety of world-class museums, including the Saint Louis Art Museum, the Contemporary Art Museum Saint Louis, and the National Blues Museum.
